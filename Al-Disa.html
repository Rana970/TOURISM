Al-Disa

Al-Disa is considered an archaeological village, as it contains the facades of unfinished Nabataean tombs carved into the rock, and the remains of the walls of residential buildings. It also contains many Nabataean and Arabic inscriptions written in Kufic script. There are also some other archaeological sites that contain the remains of residential settlements, such as Al-Mushrif, the Sokhna site, and Al-Maskouna.
